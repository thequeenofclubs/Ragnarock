# Engineering Principles

## Overview
Ragnarock is built on a set of engineering principles that guide its acoustic, mechanical, and electrical design. These principles ensure that the system performs reliably, predictably, and consistently across a wide range of conditions.

---

# Acoustic Principles

## Controlled Excursion
The low-frequency driver and passive radiator are selected for their ability to handle high displacement without distortion. The DSP enforces excursion limits through frequency-dependent compression.

## Phase Coherence
The linear-phase crossover ensures that the LF and MF/HF drivers sum coherently. This improves imaging, transient accuracy, and off-axis consistency.

## Wide Dispersion
The dual 2-inch drivers provide broad horizontal and vertical dispersion. This reduces the sensitivity of the system to listener position.

---

# Mechanical Principles

## Structural Rigidity
The Aspen hardwood enclosure and internal bracing minimize panel vibration. The laminated construction increases stiffness and reduces resonance.

## Material Stability
Materials are chosen for their stability under thermal and mechanical stress. Brass baskets, carbon fiber diaphragms, and rubber surrounds maintain consistent behavior over time.

## Vibration Control
The passive radiator and LF driver are mounted to distribute mechanical forces evenly. Internal damping reduces standing waves and cabinet coloration.

---

# Electrical Principles

## Efficient Power Delivery
The Class D amplifier provides high efficiency and stable output into a 4 ohm load. The 20 V, 5 A supply defines the electrical headroom.

## Protection Through DSP
The DSP manages:
- Peak limiting
- RMS limiting
- Excursion control
- Thermal derating

These mechanisms ensure safe operation without audible artifacts.

## Gain Staging
Gain structure is optimized to prevent clipping at any stage of the signal chain. The system maintains headroom for dynamic peaks.

---

# System Integration Principles

## Predictability
The system is designed to behave consistently across environments. Tuning, crossover behavior, and protection mechanisms are chosen for stability.

## Transparency
Engineering decisions are documented clearly. Users can understand how the system works and why it behaves as it does.

## Reliability
Components are selected for long-term durability. The system is validated through stress testing, thermal cycling, and continuous playback.

---

# Design Intent
The engineering principles behind Ragnarock emphasize:
- Acoustic control
- Mechanical stability
- Electrical efficiency
- Predictable system behavior
- Long-term reliability
