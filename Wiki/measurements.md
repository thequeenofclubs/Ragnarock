# Measurement Methodology

## Overview
Ragnarock’s specifications are based on a combination of manufacturer data, empirical measurements, and controlled testing. The goal is to provide accurate, repeatable information that reflects real-world performance rather than idealized laboratory conditions.

---

# Measurement Environment

## Room Conditions
Measurements were conducted in a controlled indoor environment with moderate acoustic treatment. Reflections were minimized but not eliminated, providing a realistic representation of typical listening spaces.

## Microphone Placement
- 1 meter on-axis for primary SPL and frequency response measurements
- 4 meters for room-scale output characterization
- Nearfield measurements for driver and passive radiator behavior

Microphones were calibrated and positioned at consistent heights relative to the acoustic center of the system.

---

# Frequency Response

## Method
Frequency sweeps were performed using a calibrated measurement microphone and software capable of generating high-resolution response curves. Multiple sweeps were averaged to reduce noise.

## Interpretation
Two response ranges are reported:
- Full response: 1 to 26.7 kHz
- Linear response: 29 to 23.8 kHz

The linear range excludes regions where room interaction or system roll-off affects accuracy.

---

# Output Capability

## SPL Measurement
- Peak output: 124 dBZ
- 1 meter: 112 dBA
- 4 meters: 92 dBA

Measurements were taken using broadband test signals and music program material. Peak values represent short-term dynamic capability rather than continuous output.

---

# Driver Behavior

## Low-Frequency Driver
The Tang Band W6-1139SIF was measured in the final enclosure. Manufacturer T/S parameters were used where consistent with observed behavior. Xmax was verified through visual inspection and excursion tracking.

## Passive Radiator
The Dayton Epique E180HE-PR was measured with the installed tuning mass. The system tuning frequency of 31 Hz was confirmed through impedance sweeps and nearfield measurements.

## Mid/High Drivers
The Samsung OEM drivers lack published T/S data. Their behavior was characterized through in-system measurements and DSP tuning.

---

# Latency

## Measurement
Latency was measured by comparing input and output waveforms across different input paths:
- Analog input: 11 ms
- Bluetooth: 112 ms
- AirPlay: 2011 ms

These values represent end-to-end system latency.

---

# Design Intent
The measurement methodology prioritizes:
- Real-world accuracy
- Repeatability
- Transparency in reporting
- Consistency with user experience
